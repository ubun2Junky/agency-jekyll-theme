Program

CURRICULUM

  • INFANT

    • Infants/toddlers will nap and rest as needed.
    • I will keep your child on a familiar routine to help them adapt to their new environment.
    • Toddlers will be read to daily and also offered introduction to shapes, colors, singing, art, language, music & physical activity and outside play are just some of the behaviors that support development during this critical stage.
    • Our Baby Steps Infant/toddler program helps babies grow, gain mobility, and develop intellectual and social skills for a lifetime of success through loving interaction.
    • Toddlers are encouraged to develop friendships, self-esteem, and curiosity.

  • 2 YEARS OLD

    • Daily routines will include circle time, art, music & movement and large motor development, with inside and outside playtime (weather permitting).
    • I also focus on cooperation (sharing and taking turns) and manners.
    • Our balanced approach in the classroom engages young minds with a blend of music, art, and movement, as well as the early education fundamentals they’ll need as they continue on to preschool.
    • Children can choose from independent activities in the classroom such as language arts, math and manipulatives, blocks, science, dramatic play, and art learning centers. I will also implement small-group activities, focusing on skill-building, cooperation and observe their behavior.
    • As two-year-olds begin to exert their increasing independence, I will continue to open their eyes to a world of learning, sharing, exploring, personal hygiene, and self-expression.
    • Children will participate in quiet activities as well. After a child has had a quiet rest after lunch, they can visit learning centers or participate in individual or small-group activities until most of the children have awakened.
    • Taught alphabet and number via songs.
    • Each week a new theme is introduced

  • 3 YEARS OLD +

    • Children will participate in hands-on-learning which includes circle time, simple math, science, art, music, with motor development, nursery rhymes and fairy tales.
    • I provide an environment that will encourage children to develop social skills (working together, taking turns, conflict resolution, etc).
    • Participate in cooperating as a group.
    • Understanding social concepts such as “please” and “thank you”.
    • Learning new vocabulary and repeating words and short sentence.
    • Indentifying numbers and counting, both in English and Spanish.
    • Using all the senses to investigate the world around them, like exploring the physical properties of water, sand, wind dirt, and paper.
    • Recognizing and writing letters and words.
    • Participating in read-aloud exercises.
    • Riding tricycles, tossing balls, dancing, and jumping.
    • Working on fine-motor skills like pouring, cutting, drawing, and stringing objects.
    • I will work with the children on practicing self-help skills such as hand and face washing plus dressing and tying shoes.
    • Most children nap or rest quietly. As children wake from rest time, they can select from a variety of quiet choices, such as manipulatives, drawing, books, or puzzles until other children have awakened.
    • Children can choose from independent activities in the classroom such as language arts, math and manipulatives, blocks, science, dramatic play, and art learning centers. I will also implement small-group activities, focusing on skill-building, cooperation and observe their behavior.
    • Children are given chores to clean up simple areas, such as class library books, listening center, and manipulatives centers.
    • Lots of effort is emphasized for our preschoolers to gain more self-esteem, and have that confidence by providing them activities to help children become problem solvers and lifelong learners.
    • Practice writting letters, number and the childs name.
    • Are taught sign language and Spanish basics.
    • Each week a new theme is introduced
